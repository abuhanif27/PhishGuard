Tips for Identifying Phishing URLs

Check for HTTPS

Legitimate websites typically use HTTPS encryption. Look for the padlock icon in your browser's address bar.

Examine the URL Carefully

Phishing sites often use URLs that look similar to legitimate ones but with slight misspellings or different domains (e.g., "amaz0n.com" instead of "amazon.com").

Be Wary of URL Shorteners

Short URLs (bit.ly, tinyurl.com, etc.) can hide the actual destination. Use our scanner to check where they lead.

Watch for Suspicious Subdomains

Phishers may use subdomains to trick you, like "paypal.suspicious-site.com" where "suspicious-site.com" is the actual domain.
